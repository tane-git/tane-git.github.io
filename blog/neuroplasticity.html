Neuroplasticity and the growth mindset

  1. Research Neuroplasticity
    • Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people
      • Believing in it in the first place makes a fundamental difference to someone's potential.
      • It reinforces the common saying “Practice makes perfect” - allowing people to realise that while they may not be naturally gifted in some domain, they still have the potential to become really good at it.
    • Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit
      • I will believe that I can become really good at programming and work towards that goal. I will practice, practice, practice, to reinforce neural networks responsible for my skills.
    • What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?
      • Keep an open mind! Don’t get stuck in one way of thinking because it doesn’t have to be the only way
      • Exercise - a huge proportion of our brain is responsible for moving our bodies, if we stop moving and become too sedentary, this part of our brain will deteriorate.
      • Just believing in neuroplasticity will increase your neuroplasticity. Remember that practice can make perfect!
    • Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.
      • 10 principles of Neuroplasticity explained in less than 1 minute each
      • I found that this video covered a lot of ground quickly (especially if you watch it at x1.5 speed).
      • I found it bizarrely relatable as I am a snowboarder (and the video used snowboarding as an example throughout) and I have also used squatting as a way to prepare for the snowboarding season, but found picking up skateboarding much more helpful!
  2. Research Growth Mindset
    • Discuss what it is and why it is relevant
      • When confronted with a difficult challenge, people react on a spectrum of;
        • This is far too hard for me, I quit, to...
        • This is far too hard for me, how exciting, how do I get better?
      • The people that react with the motivation to improve and therefore step up to solve the challenge, grow more, than those that run from the challenge.
      • It is relevant to everyone as we all face challenges and we decide to run or face them. If it is a good challenge (although hard) then we should face it, and realise that we just can’t solve it ‘yet’ but that it is not impossible.
    • In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?
      • Not really surprising as I think I had always known this subconsciously, however it is nice to hear it explained with a backing of scientific research and explanation. The change will be moving it from subconscious to conscious awareness.
    • How will you integrate a growth mindset into your learning journey?
      • Work at a speed that challenges me and forces me to grow.
      • Put in the required effort to face, understand and solve problems that are too hard for my current abilities. Stimulating growth.
      • Encourage my team mates to grow, and vice versa.
