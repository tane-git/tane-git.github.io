In summary, the strengths and areas for development I identified from this personality assessment tool are:

Introverted: I am comfortable alone with my own thoughts, which helps me stay focussed and find flow when working through a difficult problem solo. This does not limit my ability to work well in a team!

Intuition: I enjoy being open-minded and exploring future possibilities. Tech will be an awesome industry to do this however, at the same time there will be procedures and standards that I will have to familiarise myself with. Being Observant of what has happened in the industry already and respecting its reasons for existence will be an important area for development.

Thinking: I am good at using logic. I refined this while completing a degree in Structural Engineering. An area for development will be in learning how to incorporate my feelings more in a good way, when making decisions.

Tactics: I have strengths in both Judging and Prospecting Tactics. I have shown evidence of being both good at planning and staying flexible. My main focus area for development is in improving the harmony between these two sides of the spectrum in my life as whole. So that my work is not ruled by plans while my free time is awash with randomness.

The personality test helped me identify some values that are important to me.

  • Truth - and therefore honesty. While objective truth is very mysterious to us, I still value all the truth I can get and find it hard to follow ideologies and hard to become motivated just by stories.
  • Love - care for others and oneself
  • I value Working Hard and Smart, not just for the sake of it, but for Meaning. A Meaning supported by Truth and Love.

  1. Using the evidence gathered, describe your core personal values.
  • Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.
  1. The choice of how to spend my time will be an on-going ethical decision until I die.
  2. How should I spend my time? I am surrounded by distractions and things that will make me momentarily happy. There are millions of people playing video games right now and it would be awesome to go and join them. And there ’s nothing I would prefer to be doing, than riding some mountain bike trails in native bush or snowboarding down mountains.
  3. But there are billions of people who are struggling just to live. How can I go off on care-free bike rides or zone-out playing video games when countless people are starving each day.
  4. I have chosen to pursue a career in software as I am hoping it will allow me to make a positive impact on the world without having to completely sacrifice my own time.
  5. The values involved in this on-going ethical situation are Truth and Love. The consequences are unravelling.
  • Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.
  1. I was raised in an atheist household. As someone that often found themselves thinking about life 's big questions, I was often extremely jealous of my classmates that had a religion to give them great meaning in their life.
  2. I enjoyed discussing with my friends that were religious, what they believed in. It was interesting hearing comforting stories of afterlife adventures when I had always just been told “Nothing ”when I asked what happened after death. However, the stories never gripped or convinced me and sometimes I was left feeling pretty nihilistic.
  3. As I grew up, I found ways to cope with the scary implications of my lack of beliefs and always found the concept of Truth, though scary at first, oddly comforting. Despite not being religious myself, I am very open-minded and can have great respect for people 's beliefs, however I can also be very critical of them internally.
  4. In terms of my values and identity. I value Truth and Love. I don ’t identify as an atheist, as I don ’t have any answers, nor do I spend any time trying to change people 's beliefs, preferring to attempt to understand them.  
  5. If anything, I identify as a super-smart-monkey/dumb-human lost in space?
  6. I recognise that the culture I was raised in, has programmed me to value money and social status even though these are just made up.
  1. Using the evidence gathered, describe your strengths and limitations.
  • Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development
  1. As discussed above in my evidence, based on the Myers Briggs based personality test I took; My strengths and limitations are:
  1. Introvert: I am comfortable alone with my thoughts which is a strength when tasked with solving tough problems on my own. However, this is rarely the best way to solve great problems, where team cooperation is essential.
  2. Intuition: I am open-minded and imaginative which will be great for the flipped learning style of this course and the innovative industry of tech. However, I will need to ensure that I recognise the reasons for current procedures and standards and give them due respect.
  3. Thinking: I am good at thinking logically which can make working with code very enjoyable. Although most code is written by humans, with human emotions which I will need to understand to some degree to be the best coder I can!
  4. Judging~Prospecting: I can plan and be flexible in different areas of life, but I need to work on being good at both of them simultaneously!
  • Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.
  1. My strengths of flexibility and time-management will help me in my learning journey and will intersect learning obstacles if I focus on improving them by practicing them! I can use time-management to ensure that I do not get overburdened with work, while still giving myself time for rest and exercise. While I can use flexibility to make the most of good opportunities to self lead my learning throughout the course and my future career!
  • Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.
  1. Throughout university there were many large group projects where some people would seem to put no effort in. At the time I would try and inspire them by delegating them  tasks  that  I would enjoy (or at least wouldn ’t hate), thinking that most engineers are very similar. It often wasn ’t very effective and would leave me thinking: why didn ’t you take that opportunity to contribute to the project when you were given it? I would have, most people would have?
  2. In reflection, I now realise how different everyone is. We all have different strengths and limitations and different tasks and appealing/daunting to different people. Giving people the freedom to choose how they contribute to a project is difficult when delegating tasks and leading a team, but it is very important.
